How NYC's Super Skinny Skyscrapers Stack Up Next to the World's Tallest Buildings

“Slenderness” is an actual term by engineers that historically describes a structure with a 1:10 or 1:12 ratio when comparing a building’s width to its height. In the last few years, newer buildings have been able to achieve even more jaw-dropping ratios. The Nordstrom Tower going up on 57th Street has a ratio of 1:23! This will make it the most slender building in the world.
